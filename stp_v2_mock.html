Phase 3 — Primary Driver Protocols

Masseter Release

Daily · 8 minutes · Jaw and TMJ

The masseter is the strongest muscle in the body relative to its size — and in most people with TMJ-driven tinnitus, it carries sustained tension they are completely unaware of.

Personalised for your profile

Your assessment identified your right masseter as the dominant side. You may find the technique produces a stronger response on the right — this is expected and the correct area to focus on.

How to do it

Using two or three fingers, apply firm circular pressure to the masseter muscle. Work through three positions systematically: front portion, middle belly, and rear attachment near the ear. 90 seconds in each position. The pressure should feel significant but not sharp.
